A Workshop on Topological Order

at University of Tübingen

Topological phases have surged in the last three decades as an essential part of modern physics, thanks to remarkable breakthroughs such as the fractional quantum Hall effect, the discovery of topological insulators, or the demonstration of non-abelian exchange statistics on a quantum computer. The subject naturally bridges fields as diverse as condensed matter physics, quantum field theory, algebraic topology, and quantum information theory.

This workshop aims at addressing fundamental questions pertaining to topological phases from a mathematical perspective. It will offer lectures and a presentations of state-of-the-art results on topics such as the classification of topological phases through tensor networks, Topological Quantum Field Theories and Topological order in Fermionic systems

The Workshop

The workshop will last for two weeks, with each week featuring two series of lectures. Each lecture series will consist of five one-hour sessions, focusing on topics like topological order in tensor networks, TQFT, fermionic systems and implementations.

Additionally, there will be several invited talks presented by field experts. Lastly, participants are encouraged to deliver contributed talks and engage in a weekly poster session.

Venue

Tübingen is an enchanting city located in the southwest of Germany, well-known for its rich history and as the home of one of the oldest universities in Europe. It was founded in the 12th century and features a charming old town filled with medieval buildings, cobblestone streets and lovely squares.

How to arrive

If you plan to travel by air, the nearest airport to Tübingen is Stuttgart Airport (STR). Upon arrival, you can conveniently reach the city center of Tübingen in approximately 45 minutes by boarding buses X82 or 828, which provide direct transportation.

When traveling by train, Tübingen offers its own centrally located train station called Tübingen Hauptbahnhof. Situated in the heart of the city, this station provides convenient connections to various cities in Germany and Europe.

The Campus

The workshop will take place at the Morgenstelle Campus, situated in the northwest of the city. This location can be conveniently reached from the city center using public transportation, particularly via the bus stop BG Unfallklinik. The campus offers workshop participants numerous opportunities, including access to modern facilities for discussions and the nearby Mensa Canteen, providing a range of dining options.
