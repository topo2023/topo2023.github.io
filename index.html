A Workshop on Topological Order

At University of Tübingen

Topological phases of matter have garnered significant interest over the past 30 years, offering a unique blend of condensed matter physics, quantum field theory, algebraic topology and quantum information theory. The intrinsic mathematical nature of numerous fundamental questions pertaining to topological phases motivates us to seek a comprehensive understanding through the application of rigorous mathematical foundations. This workshop aims to address these challenges by offering a series of lectures on selected mathematical topics and presenting state-of-the-art results in the field.

Venue

Tübingen is an enchanting city located in the southwest of Germany, well-known for its rich history and as the home of one of the oldest universities in Europe. It was founded in the 12th century and features a charming old town filled with medieval buildings, cobblestone streets and lovely squares.

Getting to Tübingen is surprisingly simple. If you're arriving by plane, the closest airport to Tübingen is Stuttgart Airport (STR). Once you land, simply hop on buses X82 or 828, and they will transport you directly to the city center of Tübingen in around 45 minutes.

If you prefer to travel by train, the city has its own train station Tübingen Hauptbahnhof, nestled in the heart of the city, offering convenient connections to several notable cities in Germany and Europe.

The workshop is scheduled to be held at the Morgenstelle Campus, which is located in the northwest of the city and surrounded by beautiful forests. It is easily accessible from the city center through public transportation, specifically the bus stop BG Unfallklink. The campus provides various opportunities for workshop participants, including access to modern facilities. Additionally, attendees will have the option to enjoy meals at the Mensa Canteen.
