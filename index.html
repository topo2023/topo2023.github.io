TOPO

A Workshop on the Mathematical Aspects of Topological Order

At University of Tübingen, Germany

Topological phases of matter have attracted tremendous attention over the last 30 years. Topological order offers a quite unique interplay between condensed matter physics, quantum field theory, algebraic topology, and quantum information theory. Although many central questions raised regarding topological phases are mathematical in character, the field still lacks firm mathematical foundations. The aim of the workshop is twofold: (i) to offer a series of lectures on selected topics of mathematical character, (ii) to expose state of art results in topological phases.

Venue

Welcome to Tübingen! This beautiful city located in the southwest of Germany is known for its historic charm and student atmosphere. Founded in the 12th century, Tübingen features an old town full of medieval buildings, cobblestone streets, and picturesque squares. It is also surrounded by the stunning landscape of the Black Forest and the mountains of the Swabian Jura. Tübingen is also home to one of the oldest universities in Europe, giving the city a vibrant academic community and a lively cultural scene. We hope you enjoy exploring Tübingen as much as we do!

Getting to Tübingen is easy, with convenient options by air, train, or car. If you're arriving by plane, the closest airport is Stuttgart Airport (STR), which is located just 30 minutes away by car or 45 minutes away by train. From the airport, you can take the S-Bahn S2 or S3 lines to Stuttgart Hauptbahnhof (main train station), and then transfer to a regional train that will take you directly to Tübingen.

If you're coming by train, Tübingen has its own train station located in the city center, with connections to many major cities in Germany and Europe. If you're driving, Tübingen is easily accessible from the A81 autobahn, which connects Stuttgart and Singen. The city also has several parking garages and lots available in and around the city center.
